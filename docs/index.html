2023 Essay Competition on Global Issues

ECGI is a youth-led essay competition that is welcome to students ranging from primary to high school students around the world. Being inspired by the United Nationsâ€™ 17 Sustainable Development Goals, our aim is to encourage the young generation to voice out their beliefs, values, and prospects regarding current affairs and sustainable development.

Entering our competition enables our participants to develop critical analysis, logical reasoning, and creative thinking skills, while demonstrating open-mindedness and global consciousness as global citizens.

UN's SDG Goals

Competition Details

Genre

Opinion piece: An opinion piece for participants to express their personal views on any global issue of their interest

Dates

Submissions opened: August 5
DEADLINE OF SUBMISSIONS: September 15th
Marking: September 16 - 27
Results: September 29

Themes and prompts

Requirements Year 4-6 (Grade 3-5) Year 7-9 (Grade 6-8) Year 10-13 (Grade 9-12)
Word count 200-500 400-800 600-1000
Themes/Prompts
  • No Poverty
  • Zero Hunger
  • Climate Change
  • Gender Equality

Select one of the SDGs as your prompt
You may also write about any other global issues identified here

Guidelines of writing
  1. What is the issue?
  2. Why is it an issue
  3. What is your opinion towards this issue
  4. Why is it significant to you?
  5. What can be done about it
  6. Final thoughts

*This is just a guideline, not a strict structure for the participants to follow

There are no guidelines for Years 7-13

Prizes

There are prizes for the top three (1st, 2nd, and 3rd place) best essays in every year category: Year 4-6, Year 7-9, Year 10-13. There will also be an overall grand prize for the best essay written among all year categories.

Marking procedure, process, and criteria

  • Clear presentation of information and focus on answering the question/ theme
  • Academic level of essay and demonstration of understanding at an insightful level
  • Creativity and originality in approach to the question
  • Use of examples and evidence
  • Range, credibility, and validity of sources used
  • Spelling, grammar, and punctuation

Terms and Conditions

  • The competition will run until September 15th, 11:59pm BJT. Late entries will not be accepted.
  • The competition is open to students aged 10~18 of any gender from any country.
  • Each participant can submit a maximum of one work for each category. Only individual entries will be accepted.
  • Entries must be original and written in English. Any form of plagiarism will be an academic integrity issue and will result in automatic disqualification.
  • If any sources are used, it should be referenced using a standard referencing system (MLA, APA, Harvard).
